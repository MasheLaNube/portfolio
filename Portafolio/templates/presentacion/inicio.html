About Me

I am Marcela López (But you can call me Mashe), currently pursuing a PhD degree in Astronomy and planetary sciences

My primary research interests encompass the study of exoplanets, planetary system modeling, and light curve analysis. I am particularly interested in expanding my expertise into Solar System science, exploring the intersection between exoplanetary research and our own planetary system. Additionally, I have conducted research on variable stars, with a paper currently under review for publication.

Research Interests

  • Exoplanetary atmospheres and characterization
  • Observational analysis of variable stars
  • Solar System dynamics and compositional studies
  • Planetary system dynamics and evolution
  • Photometric and spectroscopic monitoring of pulsating stars
  • Compositional analysis and dynamics of Solar System bodies

Research Experience

MOTE project researcher – Monitoring of transiting exoplanets, focusing on the development and application of tools for data analysis and curve cleaning. Working with data from TESS and SPECULOOS. VVVX Science Team member – Studies of star clusters, variable searches and low-mass stars.

Current Projects

REBECCA: A comprehensive pipeline for real-time TESS data extraction and cleaning.
VVVX Research project: Analysis of variable stars in the VVVX survey, focusing on low-mass stars.
